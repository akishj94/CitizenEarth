Antarctica

Antarctica is the kind of place where everything makes sense again.

where scale humbles you, silence teaches you, and the horizon reminds you that we belong to something far greater than ourselves.

Antarctica is the coldest, wildest, most climate-sensitive frontier of the planet and is warming nearly four times faster than the global average.
Filmmakers, scientists, entrepreneurs, and storytellers from across the world came together to understand something fundamental: what happens in the most remote parts of the planet shapes life everywhere else.
We realised that distance is an illusion. Interconnection is not.
Saanya was selected as a Youth Ambassador for the 2041 ClimateForce Antarctica Expedition on leadership, climate change and sustainability, led by polar explorer Sir Robert Swan OBE - the first person in history to walk to both the North and South Poles!

The polar expeditions were live masterclasses. On ice that belongs to no one, borders feel theoretical and systems feel shared. What remains is interdependence. We understood how tightly everything is woven - oceans shaping weather, ice influencing food systems, decisions in boardrooms affecting coastlines.
Belonging isn't sentimental.
It's structural.

Our Logo/Brand Mascot

Ferð / The Arctic Tern /
[ferth]•Icelandic
(n.) journey; trip; voyage; expedition; travel.
A word rooted in movement across vast, elemental landscapes shaped by wind, ice and sea. In Icelandic, ferð is not simply travel - it is the act of setting out with intention.
The Arctic Tern is the greatest migrator on the planet, traveling from the Arctic to Antarctica and back each year - covering close to 70,000 kilometers annually. It carries memory, perspective and survival across extremes. Flying above glaciers and open oceans, it moves between fragile ecosystems that are changing faster than ever.
Like our founder's journey
from Antarctica to the Arctic
it represents lived experience at the edges of the world.
The tern does not remain in one place.
  • It observes.
  • It adapts.
  • It returns with knowledge.
